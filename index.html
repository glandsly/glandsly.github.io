Summary: Housing Market in Texas

Welcome to our Texas Housing Market Analysis! For this project, we sourced our data from Texas A&M. We used this data to analyze the following relationships:

  • Sales per Total Listing ona monthly and annual basis (inclusive of YoY percent change)
  • Average Sale Price by Year per County (inclusive of YoY percent change)
  • Average Sale Price by Year across Counties
  • Average Sale Price by County using a Box and Whisker plot
  • Average Median Sale Price by County using a map view

Each visualization may be viewed by navigating to the images on the right, or by clicking on the dropdown lists in the header above!

Map Visual

Using the map visual above, we discovered that the counties with the highest median price (darkest blue) over the past five years (2015-2020) are:

  1. Travis County (Austin): $333,261
  2. Collin County: $314,595
  3. Denton County: $288,537
  4. Comal County: $278,172
  5. Fortbend County: $276,796

The most inexpensive county (light blue) to purchase a home in Texas is Wichita County; for an average of $116,205 over the past five years.
